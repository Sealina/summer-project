Yoneyama Mai

I found myself drawn to her work because of the way she combines colors and how she has truly mastered lighting in her pieces. Her distinct style makes her art even more captivating. She is able to draw simple expressions yet convey emotions like happiness and sadness, making her art feel very alive. I think I can learn a lot from her art, especially how she creates and designs each character, giving them their own unique identity and avoiding the same face syndrome.

Pen name: Moja Cookie

I’m inspired by their illustrations, especially the way they design their animal characters. While they create a variety of animals, they are best known for a particular series of cats, especially a white cat character that scolds you for your everyday mistakes. I didn’t realize it was possible to show so much expression in a simple cat drawing. You can clearly feel the emotions of the characters without any words. Their style is simple, yet often funny and relatable at the same time.

Guri Ming

I am inspired by her art because I really like her style. It’s not realistic, but it’s not too cartoonish either. She draws people in a way that feels cute and relatable, often capturing everyday life. I also love the fashion choices of her characters, which make the scenes feel more realistic and stylish. The color palette she uses is soft and pastel, which matches with my own art.
